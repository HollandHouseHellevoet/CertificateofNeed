# Alabama Healthcare Takedown Report

**Investigation Date**: November 21, 2025
**Author**: Manus AI

## Overview

This comprehensive investigation exposes the deep contradictions within Alabama's healthcare system, where a Republican-dominated government that espouses free-market principles actively cultivates and protects a network of wealthy, tax-exempt hospital monopolies.

## Key Findings

1. **CON Laws**: Alabama maintains one of the nation's most restrictive Certificate of Need regimes, requiring government permission for 10 different service categories.

2. **UAB's Medicaid Monopoly**: UAB Health System controls Medicaid care coordination for 6 of 7 regions (60 of 67 counties), creating massive vertical integration.

3. **340B Profiteering**: 100% of Alabama's 340B disproportionate share hospitals earn more in 340B profits than they spend on charity care.

4. **Million-Dollar Executives**: UAB CEO earned $2.6 million while the entity reported a $15.9 million loss. A UAB cardiologist made $3.8 million.

5. **Tax-Exempt Billions**: Major hospital systems own billions in tax-exempt real estate while providing below-average charity care.

## The Contradiction

**Alabama's Political Rhetoric**:
- Free markets
- Limited government
- Competition
- Rural healthcare access

**Alabama's Hospital Reality**:
- Government-granted monopolies (CON laws)
- Billions in tax exemptions
- Government-funded drug discounts (340B)
- Government contracts (Medicaid ACHN)
- No accountability or minimum standards
- Million-dollar executive pay at "nonprofits"

## Call to Action

1. **Repeal Alabama's Certificate of Need laws**
2. **Reform the 340B program** to ensure benefits flow to patients
3. **End the UAB/Viva Health monopoly** on Medicaid care coordination
4. **Re-evaluate property tax exemptions** for multi-billion dollar "nonprofit" systems
