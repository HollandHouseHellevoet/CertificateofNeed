# West Virginia CON Law Political Battle - The "Freedom State" Hypocrisy

## Current Status (2025)

### Governor Patrick Morrisey's Position
**Republican Governor Patrick Morrisey** made CON repeal a **top priority** in his State of the State address (January 2025):

> "CON is big government activism at its worst"

> Promised by repealing it, the state would "move toward the free market"

### Legislative Outcome: DEFEATED

**House Bill 2007** - CON repeal bill
- **Voted down 13-12** by House Health Committee (February 24, 2025)
- Lost by a single vote
- This was the **second time** the House Health Committee killed CON repeal (also defeated in 2022, 12-10)

**Senate bill** still pending as of February 2025, but House defeat is a major blow.

## The Hypocrisy: "Freedom State" That Protects Monopolies

### West Virginia's Self-Image
- Claims to be a "freedom state"
- Republican supermajority: 91 Republicans, 9 Democrats in House of Delegates
- 32 Republicans, 2 Democrats in State Senate
- Republican Governor campaigning on "free market" principles

### The Reality
- **CON laws remain in place** despite Republican control
- Hospital monopolies protected by government regulation
- New healthcare providers must get **permission from competitors** to enter market
- "Big government activism" continues despite rhetoric

## Who Killed CON Repeal?

### The West Virginia Hospital Association (WVHA)

**WVHA is the lobbying arm of incumbent hospitals**, including:
- WVU Medicine
- Charleston Area Medical Center (CAMC/Vandalia Health)
- Other established hospital systems

**WVHA's Arguments Against Repeal:**
1. CON protects rural and vulnerable populations
2. Repeal would allow "cherry-picking" by new providers
3. 75% of WV residents are "government payers" (Medicare, Medicaid, PEIA)
4. "Out-of-state interests" want to "fleece" West Virginia
5. CON ensures "community planning" and prevents "dangerous" competition

### Cardinal Institute's Rebuttal

**Cardinal Institute for West Virginia** (free-market think tank) exposed WVHA's arguments as "elite protectionism":

> "What the President of the West Virginia Hospital Association won't tell you is that CON laws don't exist to ensure access or quality. They exist to block competition and shield providers from accountability."

**Key points:**
1. **West Virginia hospitals are failing patients**: Spring 2025 Leapfrog report showed WV hospitals failed at basic activities like handwashing, safe medication administration, preventing patient falls
2. **CON protects inefficiency**: Hospitals don't improve because they're insulated from competition
3. **"Cherry-picking" argument is backwards**: CON limits patient access, especially in rural areas
4. **Innovation is blocked**: Doctors and nurses can't expand services without permission from entrenched interests
5. **Data proves CON repeal works**: States that repealed CON saw new investments in healthcare, not deterioration

## The Vote Breakdown

### House Health Committee Vote (Feb 24, 2025)
- **Against repeal (protect CON)**: 13 votes
- **For repeal (end CON)**: 12 votes
- **Margin**: Lost by 1 vote

### Notable Details:
- **Little discussion or debate** before the vote
- Committee voted down a "committee substitute" (modified version) of HB 2007
- Del. Mike Pushkin (D-Kanawha) called the bill "flawed" and "not well thought out"
- Pushkin proposed amendment to lift methadone clinic moratorium (denied)
- Pushkin noted methadone moratorium created a "not well regulated monopoly"

### 2022 Vote (Previous Attempt)
- **Against repeal**: 12 votes
- **For repeal**: 10 votes
- **Three members absent** (didn't vote)

## What the Modified Bill Would Have Done

The committee substitute that was voted down would have:
1. **Eliminated the Health Care Authority** (CON oversight agency)
2. **Transferred oversight to Attorney General** to monitor hospital reimbursement rates and costs
3. **Kept moratoriums** on methadone clinics, nursing homes, hospice care
4. **Required legislative action** for hospital takeovers or service expansions
5. **AG approval required** for any hospital rate increases

**This was a COMPROMISE bill** - not full repeal - and it still lost.

## Institute for Justice (IJ) Report

**IJ rates West Virginia's CON laws** in their "CONning the Competition" report:
- West Virginia is one of 36 states (plus DC) that retain CON laws
- WV CON covers multiple healthcare services
- Blocks competition and raises costs

## The "Government Payers" Argument

**WVHA claims**: 75% of West Virginia residents are "government payers" (Medicare, Medicaid, PEIA), so free market won't work.

**Cardinal Institute response**: 
> "No regulation will change that math. What will help is innovation, efficiency, and competition. Precisely what CON is designed to prevent."

**Analysis**: This argument is circular logic:
1. Government programs dominate healthcare payment
2. Therefore, government should also control supply (CON)
3. This creates a fully government-controlled healthcare system
4. Contradicts "freedom state" rhetoric

## The Real Beneficiaries of CON

2. **CAMC/Vandalia Health** - protects Charleston market dominance
3. **Other incumbent hospitals** - guaranteed market share
