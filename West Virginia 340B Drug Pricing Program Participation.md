# West Virginia 340B Drug Pricing Program Participation

## Overview

**36 West Virginia hospitals** participate in the federal 340B Drug Pricing Program as of April 2024.

The 340B Drug Pricing Program is a federal initiative established in 1992 that enables eligible hospitals and healthcare organizations to purchase outpatient medications at significantly discounted prices. The program aims to provide financial relief to healthcare providers serving vulnerable populations.

## How the Program Works

**Intended Purpose:**
- Hospitals purchase drugs at steep discounts from pharmaceutical companies
- Savings are supposed to be used to fund essential services for vulnerable populations
- Examples: free or low-cost medication assistance, expanded healthcare access, community outreach

**Actual Practice (per Wall Street Journal investigation):**
- Hospitals "often extend their 340B discounts to clinics in well-off communities"
- They charge privately insured patients MORE than Medicaid patients
- "The program appears to be bolstering profits in well-off areas more than it is underwriting services in less-privileged neighborhoods"

## Congressional Scrutiny

U.S. Sen. John Kennedy (R-La.) introduced a bill in 2023 requiring transparency:

> "The 340B Drug Pricing Program is supposed to help low-income and uninsured Americans afford their medicine, but some are taking advantage of the program at the expense of vulnerable patients."

## 340B Revenue Potential

Based on the Stanford Health case study from Michigan framework:
- Stanford Health warned that cuts to 340B would cost them "hundreds of millions"
- Stanford has 3,500+ contract pharmacy relationships statewide in California
- 340B has become a "powerful revenue engine" for large health systems

**For WVU Medicine with 20 hospitals in 340B:**
- Likely generating **tens to hundreds of millions** in 340B revenue annually
- Revenue comes from spread between discounted purchase price and full billing price
- Especially lucrative for commercially insured patients

## The 340B Profit Mechanism

1. **Hospital purchases drug at 340B discount**: e.g., $100 drug costs $50
2. **Hospital bills insurance at full price**: Bills $100 (or more with markup)
3. **Hospital keeps the spread**: $50 profit per prescription
4. **Multiply by thousands of prescriptions**: Millions in annual profit
5. **Contract pharmacies expand reach**: Hospitals partner with retail pharmacies to expand 340B footprint beyond hospital walls

### 1. WVU Medicine Dominates 340B in West Virginia

- Generates substantial "hidden" revenue stream beyond patient care

### 2. 340B as Profit Center, Not Charity
- Wall Street Journal investigation found hospitals use 340B to "bolster profits in well-off areas"
- Sen. Kennedy: "some are taking advantage of the program at the expense of vulnerable patients"
- Hospitals charge privately insured patients MORE, not less, despite 340B discounts

### 3. Lack of Transparency
- No public disclosure of 340B revenues by hospital
- No requirement to show how 340B savings benefit low-income patients
- WVU Medicine claims savings "returned to community" but provides no specifics

### 4. Contract Pharmacy Expansion
- Hospitals partner with retail pharmacies to expand 340B reach
- Allows hospitals to capture 340B discounts on prescriptions filled miles away from hospital
- Transforms community pharmacies into profit centers for hospital systems

### 5. Comparison to Stanford Health Model
- Stanford Health: "hundreds of millions" in 340B revenue
- WVU Medicine with 20 hospitals likely similar scale
- 340B revenue may exceed charity care spending by large multiples
