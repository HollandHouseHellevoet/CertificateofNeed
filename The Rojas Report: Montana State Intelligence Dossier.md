# The Rojas Report: Montana State Intelligence Dossier

**State Classification:** Reform (CON Repealed)
**Date:** March 2026
**Author:** Manus AI for The Rojas Report

## Executive Summary

Montana represents a compelling "Reform" market within the US healthcare landscape. Having successfully repealed nearly all Certificate of Need (CON) laws in 2021, the state has seen a rapid expansion of outpatient surgery centers and home health agencies [1]. Despite this regulatory freedom, the hospital market remains highly concentrated, dominated by the recently merged Billings Clinic-Logan Health system and other large non-profits [2]. The state currently has two grandfathered physician-owned hospitals (POHs), one of which—Great Falls Clinic Hospital—demonstrates the high-quality, 5-star care model that physician ownership can deliver [3]. However, the state's rural geography presents unique challenges, with 50 critical access hospitals and significant vulnerability to closures [4]. For Physician Capital and PhyCapFund, Montana offers a prime environment for ASC development and independent practice fortification, leveraging the absence of CON barriers.

## 1. Market Dominance & Consolidation

The Montana hospital market is characterized by significant consolidation, primarily driven by large non-profit systems. The top 15 hospitals in the state account for 83% of all net patient revenue (NPR) [5].

### Major Health Systems

| Health System | Key Facilities | Market Position | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Billings Clinic-Logan Health** | Billings Clinic Hospital, Logan Health Medical Center | Dominant (#1) | Merged in Sept 2023, creating the state's largest system. Dominates both south-central (Billings) and northwest (Kalispell) markets [2]. |
| **Intermountain Health** | St. Vincent Healthcare (Billings), Holy Rosary (Miles City) | Strong (#2) | Formerly SCL Health; represents significant out-of-state non-profit presence [5]. |
| **Benefis Health System** | Benefis Hospital (Great Falls) | Regional Dominance | Independent non-profit; operates the largest single hospital in the state (516 beds) [5]. |
| **Providence Health** | Providence St. Patrick Hospital (Missoula) | Regional Competitor | Part of the massive Renton, WA-based Providence system [5]. |

### Consolidation Trends & Antitrust Scrutiny

The 2023 merger of Billings Clinic and Logan Health significantly altered the competitive landscape, uniting the state's two highest-revenue hospitals [2] [5]. Logan Health (formerly Kalispell Regional Healthcare) has a history of aggressive consolidation, having taken full ownership of The HealthCenter (a former physician partnership) in 2019-2020 [6]. Notably, Kalispell Regional paid a record $24 million in 2018 to settle a whistleblower lawsuit regarding Stark Law violations and improper physician compensation [7].

In response to growing consolidation, the Montana legislature passed HB 586 in 2025, requiring prior state approval for hospital mergers and acquisitions of healthcare practices, signaling increased regulatory scrutiny of anti-competitive behavior [8].

## 2. Physician-Owned Hospital Landscape

Montana is home to two grandfathered physician-owned hospitals, though their current operational structures differ significantly.

### Great Falls Clinic Hospital (Great Falls, MT)
*   **CMS Certification Number:** 270086
*   **Status:** Active, thriving POH model.
*   **Quality:** Consistently earns a 5-star CMS rating (4th consecutive year as of 2024) [3].
*   **Ownership Structure:** Founded in 1917 as Montana's first group practice. It is currently a wholly owned subsidiary of Surgery Partners (NASDAQ: SGRY), which operates surgical hospitals in partnership with physicians [9]. In 2022, Privia Health became the majority owner of the clinical operations, while Catalyst Healthcare Real Estate acquired the physical campus for $63 million [10].
*   **Market Dynamic:** Competes directly with the dominant non-profit Benefis Health System in the Great Falls market, proving that physician-led models can deliver superior quality even against entrenched incumbents.

### HealthCenter Northwest (Kalispell, MT)
*   **CMS Certification Number:** 271314
*   **Status:** Formerly a POH partnership; now fully absorbed by a mega-system.
*   **History:** Opened in 2003 as a partnership between Kalispell Regional and Flathead Physician Group. In 2019-2020, Kalispell Regional (now Logan Health) acquired full ownership, effectively ending its independent physician-owned status [6].

## 3. Regulatory & Legislative Environment

Montana is classified as a **Reform** state due to its progressive stance on healthcare regulation, most notably the repeal of CON laws.

### Certificate of Need (CON) Repeal
In October 2021, Governor Greg Gianforte signed HB 231, repealing nearly all of Montana's CON laws [1]. The results have been overwhelmingly positive for market competition:
*   Outpatient surgery centers, home health agencies, and inpatient addiction treatment centers grew by 12.5% [1].
*   Home health companies expanded into at least 18 additional counties within a year [1].
*   This environment is highly favorable for Physician Capital clients looking to develop new ASCs or expand independent practices without bureaucratic interference.

### Payer Mix
According to 2023 KFF data, Montana's payer mix reflects its rural and aging demographic [11]:
*   **Employer/Commercial:** 42%
*   **Medicaid:** 22% (Expansion renewed in 2019, addressed again in 2025 session)
*   **Medicare:** 18%
*   **Uninsured:** 9%

### Federal Legislative Delegation
Despite the state's pro-market reforms locally, Montana's federal delegation has not yet championed national POH expansion legislation.
*   **Senators:** Steve Daines (R) and Tim Sheehy (R). Neither is currently a cosponsor of S. 1390 (Physician Led and Rural Access to Quality Care Act) [12].
*   **Representatives:** Ryan Zinke (R-MT-01, retiring) and Troy Downing (R-MT-02). Neither is a cosponsor of H.R. 2191 or H.R. 4002 [13] [14].
*   **Strategic Opportunity:** Given the state's 50 critical access hospitals and the fact that 25% of rural hospitals are at risk of closure [4], there is a strong narrative to pitch S. 1390 and H.R. 2191 to the Montana delegation as a rural healthcare preservation strategy.

## 4. Investment & Strategic Opportunities

For the Rojas Report ecosystem (Physician Capital, PhyCapFund, MedMerge), Montana presents distinct opportunities:

1.  **ASC Development (Physician Capital):** The absence of CON laws makes Montana a frictionless environment for developing new Ambulatory Surgery Centers. Independent physicians seeking to escape the gravity of the Billings Clinic-Logan Health mega-system are prime candidates for Physician Capital's fortification services.
2.  **Rural Healthcare Innovation (PhyCapFund):** With 64% of rural hospitals sustaining loss of services [4], there is a critical need for innovative, physician-led delivery models in rural Montana. Startups focusing on rural telehealth, decentralized diagnostics, or micro-hospitals could find a strong testing ground here.
3.  **MedMerge Anti-Solution:** Independent practices in Montana face immense pressure to sell to Logan Health or Intermountain. MedMerge's self-funded captive model for benefits and insurance is the exact "anti-solution" needed to keep these practices financially independent.
4.  **Media Strategy (The Rojas Report):** Great Falls Clinic Hospital should be featured on The Rojas Report as a premier case study. Interviewing their leadership on how they maintain a 5-star CMS rating while competing against Benefis, and navigating their partnership with Surgery Partners, would provide high-value content for the "CNBC of Healthcare."
